UW Study Buddy

Students at UW are provided with many opportunities to connect and meet like-minded students, whether it be through classes or RSOs. What's missing is a digital platform to find like-minded people beyond the means of working on group projects or meeting at club events! This is why we came up with UW Study Buddy, an academic social media platform meant to make...

  • finding study groups
  • making friends in your major
  • and seeking academic advice or supplies easier!

Problem Background

Identify an information technology problem in that space (e.g., "what information problems related to homelessness are unaddressed?").

  • Studying by proximity: One example of an information technology pain point that has gone relatively unaddressed in this space is creating opportunities for students on the same college campus or educational institution to form study groups, as many existing websites are not catered toward students who want to create local groups.
  • Optimizing matching algorithms: While there are some existing (albiet outdated) sites that help students find groups for startups or projects, most of these sites are not accomodating for individuals who are hoping to find friends and make deeper connections while furthering academics. Existing matching algorithms are not comprehensize enough to achieve this. For more information on the importance of group learning, visit this link.
  • Platforms not being subject-specific: Many students are seeking for connections with people who are pursuing a similar academic path. Existing platforms are not built to include the different courses within specific institutions, which hinders the ability of students to find a population who are working on the same assignments. By including a school's specific courses in a website's functionality, students will be able to find students in their same subject much more efficiently.

Existing Attempts

Identify at least 1 previous or existing attempt to solve this problem (e.g., "what other web apps attempt to address homelessness? What has already been tried?")

  • Studdy: Studdy is a networking app designed for students to match with their peers and provide a platform to give and receive help with their schoolwork while meeting new people and making friends. It was created during the Covid period from the lack of interaction witnessed between students through virtual classes. Setbacks: This app works globally and is not specific towards a certain campus, thus, it is unlikely to find lasting group of friends with similarities.
  • Study Together: Study Together is a social learning app that lets you connect with students from around the world. It allows you to join or create study groups, join community events which includes career advice and free tutor help. This has the same problem scope as the Studdy app where it doesn't target a specific campus and thus isn't a suitable app to make lasting connections on campus.
